User Guide

PlethoMove transfers files between nearby iPhone and Mac devices using Apple's MultipeerConnectivity framework.

To use the app:

  1. Install PlethoMove(for iOS) on iPhone, and install PlethoMove(for MacOS) on macbook. Open both apps on both devices.
  2. Enable (Allow to use) Wi-Fi and Bluetooth on both devices.
  3. Advertising and Browsing on both devices should start by themselves, when both apps on both devices are open.
  4. Connect the devices (Click the Blue "Connect" Button. Light up green means Connected).
  5. Select photos or videos on iPhone and send them to Mac, by clicking on the Blue "Pick from Recents. (Photos & Videos)" button.
  6. Select Photos and Videos just like Apple Photos app, allows finger sliding select, with the only difference that PlethoMove shows a banner with Estimated future Export time and Transfer Time. This allows users to know how much time future transfer might be so user can decide how many files to be selected. It also protects and not allowing further selecting of files if the files selected exceeds the remaining iPhone memory space (a Red "Maximum allowable selection reached" will show on the banner). This is to prevent any future futile transfer.
  7. Click the Blue "Add" button on top right of iPhone to start the transfering.
  8. On Macbook side, a window will pop up asking whether to accept these files transfer. Click "Accept".
  9. Transfer will start. On both iPhone and Macbook side, a progress bar will be shown to see how much percentage is transferred. Additionally, on iPhone side, the Remaining minutes to finished will be shown to user for good visibility. When the progress bar transferred 100%, the transfer is finished. The blue progress bar will also disappear, which means finished.
  10. On macbook side, once the transfer is done, click on the "Files Transferred Here" button in Blue, it will take user to the folder that contains all the files that have been transferred. Those are the final files you want.

    Another way to go to the same PlethoMove sandbox location is: On macbook Finder, go to the top menu Go, press the Option key to reveal Library in the menu, click on Library, find and click on the Containers folder, click on the PlethoMove folder, click on the Data folder, then click on the Documents folder.

    Equivalently, if using macbook Terminal, use:
    cd ~/Library/Containers/dev.neuras.plethomove/Data/Documents

    That is:
    /Users/(your_user_name)/Library/Containers/dev.neuras.plethomove/Data/Documents

    Please note: be sure to move all these final files to your desired folder outside of this PlethoMove sandbox Documents folder. PlethoMove app has a function button "Clean Up Temporary Files". Clicking on that will clean up the PlethoMove sandbox, including content in this PlethoMove sandbox Documents folder. So be sure to move your desired final transferred files to a new desired place, outside of PlethoMove sandbox Documents. See step 10.
  11. These files can then be moved to another desired folder, or to a USB disk if plugged in to macbook, with the standard select-all and copy-and-move or copy-and-paste.

    For example, first on the macbook desktop, create your desired folder, such as Trip_LakeTahoe. Then come back to PlethoMove app, click on the blue button "Files Transferred Here" (see step 9). In the Finder top menu, click Edit, then click Select All. This way all the final transferred files are highlighted.

    You can then drag all of them into your new desired folder, the Trip_LakeTahoe folder on the desktop. This way all the files are moved cleanly from the PlethoMove sandbox Documents folder to the new desired folder on desktop, Trip_LakeTahoe. This Trip_LakeTahoe folder can then be copied to an external USB disk, or backed up in any other way.
  12. Once you confirm all files are transferred successfully from iPhone to Macbook, and already saved to the new Trip_LakeTahoe folder for example, then you can open the standard Photos app on iPhone and delete the same files there to release your iPhone memory.

No login or account is required. The app works entirely on the local network and does not upload data to any server.

Both devices must be on the same Wi-Fi network with Bluetooth enabled. PlethoMove uses the local network to discover nearby devices and transfer files between your iPhone and Mac.

* Important Tips regarding power save: Consider turning off auto-lock to avoid interruption during long transfer:

iPhone Settings > Display & Brightness > Auto-Lock > Never

Macbook > System Settings > Battery > Options > Prevent automatic sleeping > turn on, and > Put hard disk to sleep > Never

* The current version of the app may be interrupted if you switch away from PlethoMove —

for example, by picking up your phone or answering calls in communication apps like WhatsApp or LINE.

* If the app becomes unresponsive, restart your iPhone.

If the issue persists, uninstall the app and reinstall it.
