User Guide

PlethoMove transfers files between nearby iPhone and Mac devices using Apple's MultipeerConnectivity framework.

To use the app:

  1. Install PlethoMove (iOS) on your iPhone and PlethoMove (macOS) on your MacBook. Then open the app on both devices.
  2. Ensure Wi-Fi and Bluetooth are enabled (allowed) on both devices.
  3. Advertising and browsing will start automatically once both apps are open on both devices.
  4. Connect the devices by clicking the blue "Connect" button. When the indicator turns green, the devices are successfully connected.
  5. Select photos or videos on your iPhone by tapping the blue "Pick from Recents (Photos & Videos)" button, then choose the files you want to send.
  6. You can select photos and videos just like in the Apple Photos app, including swipe selection. PlethoMove also displays a banner showing estimated export time and transfer time. This helps you decide how many files to select. If your selection exceeds the available iPhone storage limit, a red "Maximum allowable selection reached" warning will appear, preventing further selection and avoiding failed transfers.
  7. Tap the blue "Add" button at the top right of the iPhone screen to begin transferring.
  8. On the MacBook, a prompt will appear asking whether to accept the transfer. Click "Accept" to proceed.
  9. The transfer will begin. A progress bar will appear on both iPhone and MacBook, showing the transfer percentage. On iPhone, the estimated remaining time will also be displayed for better visibility. When the progress reaches 100%, the transfer is complete, and the blue progress bar will disappear.
  10. On the MacBook, once the transfer is complete, click the blue "Files Transferred Here" button. This will open the folder containing all transferred files. These are your final files.

    Alternatively, you can access the same PlethoMove sandbox location manually:
    In Finder, go to the top menu Go, hold the Option key to reveal Library, then click Library → Containers → PlethoMove → Data → Documents.

    Or via Terminal:
    cd ~/Library/Containers/dev.neuras.plethomove/Data/Documents

    Full path:
    /Users/(your_user_name)/Library/Containers/dev.neuras.plethomove/Data/Documents

    Important: Move all final files to a desired location outside of this PlethoMove sandbox Documents folder. The "Clean Up Temporary Files" function will remove all contents inside this sandbox, including these files.
  11. You can now move the files to another folder or an external USB drive using standard select-all and drag/copy operations.

    Example:
    Create a folder on your desktop (e.g., Trip_LakeTahoe). Then click "Files Transferred Here" (see previous step). In Finder, go to Edit → Select All to highlight all files.

    Drag all selected files into your new folder (Trip_LakeTahoe). This moves them cleanly out of the PlethoMove sandbox into your desired location. You can then back up or copy this folder to external storage.
  12. After confirming all files have been successfully transferred and saved (for example, in the Trip_LakeTahoe folder), you can open the Photos app on your iPhone and delete the original files to free up storage.

No login or account is required. The app operates entirely on the local network and does not upload data to any server.

Both devices must be connected to the same Wi-Fi network with Bluetooth enabled. PlethoMove uses the local network to discover nearby devices and transfer files.

* Important tip (power saving): To avoid interruptions during long transfers, consider disabling auto-lock:

iPhone: Settings → Display & Brightness → Auto-Lock → Never

MacBook: System Settings → Battery → Options → Enable “Prevent automatic sleeping” and set “Put hard disk to sleep” to Never

* The current version of the app may be interrupted if you switch away from PlethoMove—

for example, by picking up your phone or answering calls in communication apps like WhatsApp or LINE.

* If the app becomes unresponsive, restart your iPhone.

If the issue persists, uninstall the app and reinstall it.
